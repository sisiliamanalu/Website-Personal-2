Sustainable tourism is tourism that takes full account of its current and future economic, social, and environmental impacts, addressing the needs of visitors, the industry, the environment, and host communities.

Sustainable tourism development guidelines and management practices are applicable to all forms of tourism in all types of destinations, including mass tourism and the various niche tourism segments. Sustainability principles refer to the environmental, economic, and socio-cultural aspects of tourism development, and a suitable balance must be established between these three dimensions to guarantee its long-term sustainability.

Thus, sustainable tourism should:

  1. Economic: Ensure long-term economic benefits, provide fair opportunities, create jobs, and support poverty reduction.
  2. Environmental: Use natural resources responsibly, support ecological balance, and protect biodiversity and natural heritage.
  3. Socio-Cultural: Respect local cultures, preserve cultural heritage and traditions, and promote intercultural understanding.

Economic

Economic sustainable tourism is a form of tourism sector development and management that aims provide long-term economic benefits that are fair and equitable to all stakeholders, especially local communities, without sacrificing social, cultural, and environmental sustainability. This concept emphasizes that economic growth resulting from tourism activities must be able to the local economy, support inclusive development, and reduce disparities between social groups, while ensuring that tourism attractions are maintained for future generations.

Functions and Objectives of Sustainable Tourism Economy

  • Increasing Local Income: A sustainable tourism economy helps local communities earn income through various activities, such as homestay businesses, handicrafts, local cuisine, tour guides, and others.
  • Encouraging Economic Independence: One of the main objectives is to create economic independence for local communities so that they are not always dependent on external investors. Tourism becomes a tool to strengthen the economy of villages or remote areas.
  • Minimizing Economic Leakage: Economic leakage occurs when most of the income from tourism actually flows out of the destination (for example to multinational companies or foreign operators). In sustainable tourism, this leakage is minimized by prioritizing local products and services.

Environment

Environmental sustainable tourism is an approach to tourism development and management that aims to negative impacts on the environment and preserve the natural resources and biodiversity that form the basis of the tourist attraction itself. Environmentally sustainable tourism emphasizes the importance of maintaining ecosystem balance, using resources wisely, and promoting environmentally friendly tourism behavior.

Functions and Objectives of Sustainable Tourism Economy

  • Conserving Natural Resources: Tourism should support the preservation of forests, rivers, and wetlands, and not drastically change the landscape. This includes protecting local flora and fauna
  • Managing Waste and Pollution: Waste management from hotels, restaurants, and tourists should be carried out in a responsible manner so as not to pollute the land, water, or air.
  • Reducing Carbon Footprint : Promoting low-emission transportation, such as bicycles, electric vehicles, or walking in tourist areas, is an important part of the environmental strategy.
  • Developing Green Infrastructure : Tourism buildings and facilities (such as resorts and restaurants) should use energy-efficient technology, water recycling systems, and environmentally friendly building materials.

Social

Social sustainable tourism is an approach to the development and management of the tourism sector that focuses on welfare, cultural preservation, empowerment of local communities, and respect for humanitarian values ​​and social justice. The goal is to ensure that tourism activities do not harm local communities, but rather strengthen social identity, expand community participation, and respect the rights of all social groups, including minorities, women, and indigenous peoples.

Functions and Objectives of Sustainable Tourism Economy

  • Improving Community Social Welfare : Socially sustainable tourism must be able to improve the quality of life of local residents, including access to education, health, and other social services.
  • Strengthening Community Participation : Local communities must be involved in the planning, decision-making, and management of tourism activities so that they feel ownership and receive real benefits.
  • Protecting and Preserving Local Culture : Local culture, traditions, languages, and values ​​must be respected and preserved, not made into meaningless commercial objects. This includes preventing folklorization (the simplification of culture only for tourist consumption).
  • Maintaining Social Harmony : Tourism activities must avoid social conflicts, such as unequal access to land, exploitation of local labor, or gentrification due to the entry of large investors.

5 Top-Priority Sustainable Tourism

Curious about how sustainability practices are implemented in the 5 Super Priority Destinations?

Discover how these iconic locations are embracing eco-friendly practices, preserving cultural heritage, and promoting responsible tourism to create a balanced and sustainable future for both the environment and local communities. Explore the innovative steps taken to maintain the delicate ecosystem while providing travelers with memorable and responsible experiences.

Borobudur

Borobudur Temple stands as both a tourist attraction and a place of worship for Buddhists in Magelang Regency, Central Java. Recognized as a UNESCO World Cultural Heritage site in 1991, Borobudur Temple invites you to experience not only its architectural beauty but also the surrounding natural wonders of the Borobudur area. As you venture here, immerse yourself in the breathtaking landscapes of Puthuk Setumbu and Bukit Barade.

Lake Toba

Lake Toba in North Sumatra has been fervently promoted as one of the world's best tourist destinations in recent years. The natural beauty of Lake Toba needs no introduction, with its clear skies and captivating lake forming a picturesque combination that holds its allure. On the shores of Lake Toba lies Bukit Gibeon in Parapat, commonly known as Smile Hill. The name reflects the delightful reactions of visitors who can't help but smile upon witnessing its breathtaking panorama.

Mandalika

The Mandalika International Circuit has been proudly hosting the MotoGP Championship since 2022. Nestled in a coastal region renowned for its exotic beaches, it offers a perfect blend of thrilling racing action and picturesque landscapes. Take, for example, the famous Kuta Mandalika Beach, celebrated for its azure waters, pristine white sands, and lush green hills. The water attractions are equally exciting, featuring windsurfing, banana boat rides, and paragliding.

Raja Ampat

The crystal-clear ocean, pristine beaches, and exotic islands elevate Raja Ampat to a coveted destination for many. Recognized as one of Lonely Planet's six global destinations, it is recommended as a must-visit location in 2023 for those seeking an unwind destination. For diving enthusiasts, Raja Ampat boasts numerous diving spots waiting to be explored. Dive into the depths of Cross Wreck, offering an underwater panorama of a Japanese shipwreck from World War II.

Likupang

The next recommended marine tourism destination in Indonesia is Likupang, North Sulawesi. In Likupang, you can explore the extensive coastline of Paal Beach, the renowned underwater paradise of Pulisan Beach, and the beaches on Lihaga Island, perfect for beachside camping enthusiasts. Likupang also offers the scenic Pulisan Hill and Larata Hill. Both hills provide breathtaking views of expansive grasslands blending harmoniously with the stunning clarity of the blue sea.

Aspects of sustainable tourism in Indonesia

To achieve this goal, the Ministry of Tourism and Creative Economy/Tourism and Creative Economy Agency (Baparekraf) emphasizes four main pillars: sustainable management in the tourism business, long-term sustainable economic development, sustainable cultural preservation, and environmental protection.

Aspect Economic : These terms are central to understanding the role of tourism in boosting the economic development of a region. Economic growth through tourism involves the creation of revenue, which circulates and stimulates other sectors of the economy, such as local businesses, infrastructure, and public services.

Aspect Environment Conservation and Water Conservation These terms highlight efforts to protect natural resources, particularly water, which is a vital resource for both local communities and the tourism industry. Conservation efforts are necessary to ensure the long-term sustainability of ecosystems that attract tourists.

Aspect Social Cultural Development and Cultural Preservation These terms highlight efforts to maintain and develop local cultural practices and heritage, ensuring they remain intact even as tourism grows. Cultural preservation aims to protect traditions, languages, arts, and customs that may otherwise be at risk from external influences.
